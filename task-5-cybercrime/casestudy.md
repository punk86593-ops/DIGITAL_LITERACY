# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

**CSE0001 — Digital Literacy | Task 5 | VIT Bhopal University**

---

## What is UPI payment fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime in which fraudsters trick victims into authorising illegal money transfers through their UPI-linked bank accounts. It is one of the fastest-growing financial crimes in India, with college students and young adults being among the most frequently targeted groups.

---

## How it typically happens — step by step

1. The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, WhatsApp, or SMS, posing as a bank representative, customer care agent, or government official.
2. They claim there is an urgent issue with the victim's bank account, UPI ID, or KYC details that must be resolved immediately.
3. They send a "collect request" on the victim's UPI app, disguised as a "verification step" or "refund."
4. The victim, believing it is routine verification, enters their UPI PIN to "accept the refund" — which actually authorises payment OUT of their account.
5. The money is instantly transferred to the fraudster's account. UPI transactions are irreversible, making recovery extremely difficult.

---

## Who is typically targeted?

- First-time UPI users and college students new to digital payments
- People who have recently registered for online services or received job offers
- Elderly individuals unfamiliar with how UPI collect requests work
- Anyone who has recently listed items for sale on OLX or Quikr

---

## Consequences

- Immediate and irreversible financial loss — often thousands of rupees
- Compromise of personal and banking information
- Psychological stress and loss of trust in digital payment systems
- In severe cases, victims have lost their entire savings in a single transaction
