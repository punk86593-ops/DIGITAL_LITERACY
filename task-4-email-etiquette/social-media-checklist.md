# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**CSE0001 — Digital Literacy | Task 4 | VIT Bhopal University**

---

## ✅ Do's

1. **Think before you post** — Ask yourself: would you be comfortable if your professor, parent, or future employer saw this? If not, don't post it.

2. **Keep your profiles professional** — Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and bio. Your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles represent you to the professional world.

3. **Use privacy settings** — Review and set appropriate privacy controls on all platforms. Limit who can see your personal posts on Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat.

4. **Give credit when sharing content** — If you share someone else's work, article, or image, always credit the original creator or source.

5. **Report harmful or abusive content** — If you see cyberbullying, hate speech, or threatening content, use the platform's report feature. Don't ignore it.

---

## ❌ Don'ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** — Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or bank details on any social media platform.

2. **Don't engage with strangers who DM you unexpectedly** — Unsolicited messages offering prizes, jobs, or friendship from unknown accounts are often scams or phishing attempts.

3. **Don't post during exams or classes** — It is unprofessional and reflects poorly on your focus and discipline, especially on platforms visible to faculty.

4. **Don't spread unverified news or rumours** — Before sharing any news article or claim, verify it through a trusted source. Spreading misinformation has real consequences.

5. **Don't use social media to vent about college, faculty, or classmates** — Negative public posts about your institution or peers can harm relationships and your academic standing.

---

## 📌 Remember

> Your digital footprint is permanent. What you post today can be found years later by employers, institutions, and colleagues. Build a digital identity you are proud of.
